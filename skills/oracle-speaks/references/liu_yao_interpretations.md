# Liu Yao (六爻) Six Line Interpretations

## Overview
Liu Yao, or Six Line, is a traditional Chinese divination method that creates a hexagram of six lines. Each line represents a different aspect of the situation and can change (become its opposite) to show future development.

## Line Positions

### Line 1 (Bottom) - 初爻 - Beginning
- **Represents**: Starting point, initial conditions, foundation
- **Question**: What is the beginning of this situation?
- **Energy**: Raw, potential, hidden

### Line 2 - 二爻 - Development
- **Represents**: Early development, building momentum
- **Question**: What is developing in this situation?
- **Energy**: Growing, taking shape

### Line 3 - 三爻 - Transition
- **Represents**: Turning point, crisis, opportunity
- **Question**: What is the critical point?
- **Energy**: Unstable, transformative

### Line 4 - 四爻 - Expansion
- **Represents**: External influence, outside help
- **Question**: What external factors affect this?
- **Energy**: Receptive, responding

### Line 5 - 五爻 - Peak
- **Represents**: Main development, leadership, success
- **Question**: What is the core of this situation?
- **Energy**: Powerful, authoritative

### Line 6 (Top) - 上爻 - Completion
- **Represents**: Outcome, conclusion, future
- **Question**: Where is this heading?
- **Energy**: Culmination, final result

## Line Types

### Old Yang (9) - ⚊ changing to ⚋
**Meaning**: Strong energy that will transform
**Interpretation**: Current strength will give way to flexibility

### Young Yang (8) - ⚊ static
**Meaning**: Stable strength
**Interpretation**: Consistent power, reliable support

### Young Yin (7) - ⚋ static
**Meaning**: Stable receptivity
**Interpretation**: Passive support, hidden potential

### Old Yin (6) - ⚋ changing to ⚊
**Meaning**: Receptive energy that will transform
**Interpretation**: Current passivity will give way to action

## Reading Technique

### Step 1: Identify the Pattern
Look at the six lines from bottom to top:
- **Mostly Yang**: Active, assertive situation
- **Mostly Yin**: Passive, receptive situation
- **Mixed**: Balanced but complex situation

### Step 2: Note Changing Lines
- **No changing lines**: Situation is stable
- **One changing line**: Specific area of transformation
- **Multiple changing lines**: Major transition occurring

### Step 3: Calculate Related Hexagram
Change all "old" lines to their opposites to get the future development.

### Step 4: Interpret the Transition
- **Main hexagram**: Current situation
- **Related hexagram**: Future development
- **Changing lines**: Areas of transformation

## Common Patterns

### All Yang Lines (乾 Heaven)
**Current**: Maximum activity, leadership, success
**Future**: May become too rigid, needs flexibility

### All Yin Lines (坤 Earth)
**Current**: Maximum receptivity, nurturing
**Future**: May become too passive, needs initiative

### Alternating Lines
**Current**: Dynamic balance
**Future**: Stable development

### Changing Middle Lines (3rd and 4th)
**Current**: Critical transition point
**Future**: Major transformation

## Line Combination Meanings

### Two Changing Lines (Adjacent)
**Interpretation**: Related transformation in same area
**Example**: Lines 2 & 3 changing = personal development transition

### Two Changing Lines (Opposite)
**Interpretation**: Dual transformation across situations
**Example**: Lines 1 & 6 changing = beginning and end transformation

### Three Changing Lines
**Interpretation**: Major life transition
**Consider**: Which trigram is affected (upper or lower)

### Four+ Changing Lines
**Interpretation**: Complete transformation
**Consider**: The few static lines as anchors

## Practical Application

### For Decision Making
1. Identify which line represents your decision area
2. Check if it's changing (old yin/yang)
3. Interpret the change direction
4. Use guidance for timing

### For Relationship Questions
- **Line 2**: Personal feelings
- **Line 5**: Relationship dynamic
- **Line 6**: Future direction

### For Career Questions
- **Line 1**: Starting position
- **Line 4**: External opportunities
- **Line 5**: Leadership role

## Example Reading

**Hexagram**: 
Line 6: ⚊ Young Yang (static)
Line 5: ⚋ Old Yin (changing) ←
Line 4: ⚊ Young Yang (static)
Line 3: ⚋ Young Yin (static)
Line 2: ⚊ Young Yang (static)
Line 1: ⚋ Young Yin (static)

**Interpretation**:
- **Current**: Balanced situation with strong foundation (Yang bottom)
- **Critical change**: Line 5 (leadership) changing from passive to active
- **Future**: More assertive leadership position

**Guidance**:
Your current stable position is preparing you for a leadership role. The change in line 5 suggests you will need to step up and take charge. Prepare by developing your leadership skills now.
